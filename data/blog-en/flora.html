Iran's mountains host one of the richest alpine floras in the Middle East — from Alborz meadows to Zagros valleys. Knowing common species is both interesting and occasionally useful in emergencies.

Common high-altitude plants

Thyme (Avishan)

Above 2,000 m on sunny slopes. Traditional use for cough and digestion. Do not confuse with look-alikes.

Wild onion (Allium)

Recognizable smell. Used in local cuisine — only harvest where permitted.

Artemisia

Silver-grey bushes on dry ridges. Strong aroma repels insects.

Colchicum

Purple autumn flowers in alpine meadows. All parts toxic — do not ingest.

⚠️ Caution
Never eat a wild plant unless you are 100% certain of identification. Many medicinal plants have toxic look-alikes.

Ethics

  • Take photos, not plants — many species are protected
  • Stay on trail to avoid trampling fragile alpine turf
  • Report rare sightings to local conservation groups
