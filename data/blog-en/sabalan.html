Mount Sabalan in Ardabil province is Iran's third highest peak and a dormant stratovolcano. Its summit crater holds a permanent lake — one of the most photogenic summits in the country.

Main route

Most climbers approach from the northeast (Shabil hot springs area). Shelter at 3,800 m, summit push on day 2. Summer (July–August) is the standard season.

Highlights

  • Crater lake often partially frozen even in summer
  • Wildflower meadows on lower slopes in June
  • Hot springs at Shabil after the climb

Tips

  • Book shelter in peak season
  • Strong sun and cold wind at summit — full layering required
  • Combine with Alvares grasslands for a longer trip
