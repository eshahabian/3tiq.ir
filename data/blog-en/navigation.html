Your phone is dead, there is no signal, and you are unsure of the route. That scenario is more common than you think. Traditional navigation — map, compass and natural signs — can save you.

💡 Why this matters
Phones fail in cold, lose signal at altitude and break in rain. A compass and paper map never run out of battery.

Reading topographic maps

Contour lines

  • Close lines = steep slope
  • Wide spacing = gentle slope
  • Closed loops with higher values inside = summit
  • V-shaped contours pointing uphill = valley

1:50,000 scale is ideal for mountaineering — 1 cm on map = 500 m on ground.

Using a compass

  • Red needle points to magnetic north
  • Cardinal bearings: N 0°, E 90°, S 180°, W 270°
  • In Iran magnetic declination is roughly 3–5° — correct for precise work

Natural navigation

  • Midday sun: shortest shadow points north (in Iran)
  • Polaris = true north at night
  • North-facing slopes retain snow longer in Iran's mountains

Offline tools

  • Print and laminate a topo map before you leave
  • Download offline maps (Maps.me, Gaia GPS) at home
  • Carry spare batteries for GPS devices
✅ Practice first
Try map-and-compass on a familiar trail before relying on them in remote terrain.
